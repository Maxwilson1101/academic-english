# Argument Expression Patterns

## Scope

Argument expression patterns provide reusable wording for realizing common meanings in argument genres.

Use this file when generating or revising language inside an argument. For genre purposes, required stages, and selection rules, use [Argument Genre Specification](argument.md).

Replace `issue`, `topic`, `claim`, `position`, `view`, `reason`, `evidence`, `A`, `B`, and `X` with topic-specific content.

## General Rules

- Select expressions according to the argument's primary organizing relation: one-sided support or competing-position evaluation.
- Use exposition expressions when the writer clearly supports one position.
- Use discussion expressions when the writer presents two or more sides before reaching a resolution.
- Use topic and issue expressions to introduce the debatable subject clearly.
- Use thesis expressions to state the writer's position in an exposition.
- Use side-previewing and opposing-view expressions to present competing positions in a discussion.
- Keep placeholders semantically consistent: do not use discussion expressions when only one position is being developed.
- Adjust modality, tone, and certainty according to the strength of the evidence.

## General Argument Expressions

Use these patterns in the opening stage of an [exposition](argument.md#exposition) or [discussion](argument.md#discussion).

### Introducing The Topic Or Issue

Use for presenting a debatable topic, issue, or public controversy.

- `More and more people have come to realize that claim.`
- `Many people think that claim.`
- `Many people believe that claim.`
- `Many people would argue that claim.`
- `X plays a vital role in topic.`
- `X plays an important role in topic.`
- `X plays an essential role in topic.`
- `X plays a crucial role in topic.`
- `The issue of topic has been brought to public attention.`
- `The topic of topic is becoming more popular with the public.`
- `Topic is one of the most important issues facing the world today.`
- `Over the past few years, there has been a lot of controversy about issue.`
- `Issue is a controversial issue.`
- `There has been heated discussion over issue.`
- `Nowadays, public debate has been going on over whether claim.`
- `The question whether claim is still open for debate.`
- `Topic is a frequently discussed topic.`

## Exposition Expressions

Use these patterns in an [exposition](argument.md#exposition), where one point of view is supported.

### Stating The Thesis

Use in the Thesis stage to state the writer's position.

- `Personally, I believe that claim.`
- `I hold a positive view about topic.`
- `I am in favor of the idea that claim.`
- `I support the view that claim.`
- `This paper argues that claim.`
- `The central argument is that claim.`
- `It is worth stating at this point that claim.`
- `An important aspect of the issue is that claim.`
- `We must distinguish carefully between A and B.`

### Developing An Argument

Use in the Arguments stage to introduce or elaborate reasons.

- `The first aspect to point out is that reason.`
- `In the first place, reason.`
- `Central to the issue is claim.`
- `Many people are against position because reason.`
- `It is widely claimed that claim.`
- `It is generally believed that claim.`
- `It is widely suggested that claim.`
- `It is generally agreed that claim.`
- `It is widely maintained that claim.`
- `It is important to add that reason.`
- `Furthermore, one should not forget that reason.`

### Ordering Arguments

Use when several reasons are presented in sequence.

- `Firstly, reason. Secondly, reason. Finally, reason.`
- `There are at least three points to highlight.`
- `The first argument suggests that claim, whilst the second suggests that claim.`
- `As a final point, reason.`
- `Another important reason is that reason.`
- `A further argument is that reason.`

### Concluding An Exposition

Use in the Reiteration stage to restate the thesis and close the argument.

- `To sum up, we find that claim.`
- `To conclude, we find that claim.`
- `In summary, I would say that claim.`
- `In conclusion, I would say that claim.`
- `From what has been discussed above, it is evident that claim.`
- `The arguments presented above suggest that claim.`
- `The arguments presented above prove that claim.`
- `The arguments presented above indicate that claim.`
- `From these arguments, one could conclude that claim.`
- `From these arguments, one might conclude that claim.`
- `All of this points to the conclusion that claim.`
- `In view of these facts, it is quite likely that claim.`
- `It is clear from these observations that claim.`
- `The evidence presented in this paper has shown that claim.`
- `Taking all these factors into consideration, we can draw the conclusion that claim.`
- `Taking all these reasons into account, we can come to the conclusion that claim.`

## Discussion Expressions

Use these patterns in a [discussion](argument.md#discussion), where competing views are weighed before a resolution.

### Previewing Different Sides

Use in the Issue stage to introduce competing positions without resolving the debate immediately.

- `There are different opinions among people as to issue. Some people think that claim, while others believe that claim.`
- `Nowadays, a heated debate about issue is under way. Some people insist that claim, while others argue that claim.`
- `On the one hand, there is the firm belief that claim. On the other hand, many people are convinced that claim.`
- `Some critics suggest that claim, whereas others argue that claim.`
- `It is worth discussing the views of both sides.`
- `It is worth examining the arguments of both sides.`
- `It is worth exploring the views and arguments of both sides.`

### Offering An Opposing View

Use in the Sides stage to introduce an alternative or opposing position.

- `On the other hand, those who argue position maintain that claim.`
- `Other people oppose position on the grounds that reason.`
- `Though position offers many advantages, some people are concerned that claim.`
- `While it may be a good point that claim, some people believe that claim.`
- `One should, nevertheless, consider the problem from another angle.`
- `However, another angle on this debate suggests that claim.`
- `It cannot be denied that claim.`
- `One must admit that claim.`
- `We cannot ignore the fact that claim.`
- `It would be unfair not to mention the fact that claim.`
- `In contrast to the previous idea, an alternative perspective illustrates that claim.`

### Ordering And Contrasting Sides

Use when moving between competing positions.

- `On the one hand, claim. On the other hand, claim.`
- `If on the one hand it can be said that claim, the same is true for claim.`
- `The first position suggests that claim, whilst the second suggests that claim.`
- `By contrast, supporters of position argue that claim.`
- `However, opponents of position maintain that claim.`
- `A different perspective is that claim.`

### Concluding A Discussion

Use in the Resolution stage to review the debate and state the final position.

- `Having considered both views on issue, we may conclude that claim.`
- `There is some truth in both arguments, but I think the disadvantages of A outweigh its advantages.`
- `There is some truth in both arguments, but I think the advantages of A outweigh its disadvantages.`
- `Therefore, it is clear that this topic is food for thought, but there are reasons to believe that claim.`
- `In summary, both A and B have benefits, but A is more useful for purpose. Therefore, I recommend A for purpose.`
- `After weighing both sides, it seems more reasonable to conclude that claim.`
- `Although both positions have strengths, position is more convincing because reason.`

## Evaluation Checks

- Does the expression match the selected [argument genre](argument.md#genre-types)?
- Does the expression preserve the argument's primary organizing relation?
- Are placeholders replaced with specific, domain-appropriate content?
- Are competing positions clearly separated in a discussion?
- Is the writer's thesis clear in an exposition?
- Does the conclusion or resolution follow from the reasons and evidence presented?

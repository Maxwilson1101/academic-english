# Vocabulary and Expressions: Effects of Cyber Bullying on Teenagers

## Topic Type

This topic fits a **consequential explanation** because it explains the possible effects produced by one phenomenon: **cyber bullying**.

Basic causal pattern:

```text
cyber bullying -> multiple possible effects on teenagers
```

## Academic Sentence Patterns

### Introducing the Phenomenon

- Cyber bullying refers to repeated harmful behavior carried out through digital platforms.
- Cyber bullying can have several serious effects on teenagers' emotional, social, and academic lives.
- Because teenagers often depend on online communication, cyber bullying may affect both their digital and offline experiences.
- The effects of cyber bullying can extend beyond the screen and influence teenagers' well-being, relationships, and school performance.

### Introducing Effects

- One major effect of cyber bullying is emotional distress.
- Another possible consequence is social withdrawal.
- A further result is a decline in academic performance.
- In some cases, cyber bullying may also affect teenagers' physical health.
- Over time, repeated online harassment may damage teenagers' confidence and sense of safety.

### Explaining How an Effect Happens

- This occurs because harmful messages can make teenagers feel ashamed, anxious, or unsafe.
- This effect follows from the constant nature of online communication, which makes it difficult for victims to escape harassment.
- As a result, teenagers may avoid school, social events, or online spaces.
- Consequently, victims may lose confidence in themselves and become less willing to interact with peers.
- This can affect learning because emotional stress may reduce concentration and motivation.

### Showing Possibility and Caution

- may lead to
- can result in
- is likely to increase
- may contribute to
- can make teenagers more vulnerable to
- may place teenagers under greater pressure
- is often associated with
- could have a negative effect on
- may make it harder for teenagers to

### Linking Multiple Effects

- In addition to emotional harm, cyber bullying may also disrupt teenagers' social relationships.
- Beyond psychological distress, the problem can affect school performance.
- These effects are connected because emotional pressure can reduce both social confidence and academic focus.
- While the immediate effect may be embarrassment, the long-term consequence may be lower self-esteem.
- The effects may become more serious when bullying is repeated or publicly visible.

## Consequential Explanation Structure

### Phenomenon Stage

Use this stage to identify cyber bullying as the cause or phenomenon.

Example:

```text
Cyber bullying refers to repeated harmful behavior, such as insulting, threatening, excluding, or spreading rumors about someone through digital platforms. This phenomenon can produce several negative effects on teenagers because online attacks may be constant, public, and difficult to escape.
```

### Explanation Stage

Organize the body by effects, not by causes.

Possible phases:

- Effect 1: emotional and psychological distress
- Effect 2: social isolation and damaged relationships
- Effect 3: lower academic performance
- Effect 4: physical and behavioral problems
- Effect 5: long-term effects on self-esteem and identity

## Model Paragraph Frames

### Effect: Emotional Distress

```text
One major effect of cyber bullying is emotional distress. When teenagers receive hurtful messages or become the target of online humiliation, they may feel anxious, ashamed, or helpless. Because online content can be shared quickly and viewed by many people, the victim may feel that the problem is impossible to escape. As a result, cyber bullying can seriously damage teenagers' emotional well-being.
```

### Effect: Social Isolation

```text
Another possible consequence is social isolation. Teenagers who experience cyber bullying may begin to mistrust their peers or avoid group chats, social media, and school activities. This withdrawal can weaken friendships and reduce their sense of belonging. Consequently, victims may feel lonely even when they are surrounded by classmates.
```

### Effect: Academic Performance

```text
Cyber bullying may also affect academic performance. Emotional stress can make it difficult for teenagers to concentrate in class or stay motivated to study. Some victims may avoid school because they fear meeting the people involved in the bullying. As a result, their attendance, participation, and grades may decline.
```

### Effect: Physical and Behavioral Problems

```text
In some cases, cyber bullying can also lead to physical and behavioral problems. Teenagers under constant online pressure may experience sleep problems, headaches, tiredness, or changes in appetite. They may also become irritable or spend excessive time checking messages. These changes show that online harassment can affect both mental and physical health.
```

### Effect: Long-Term Self-Esteem

```text
Over time, repeated cyber bullying may damage teenagers' self-esteem. If victims are constantly criticized, mocked, or excluded online, they may begin to believe negative ideas about themselves. This can reduce their confidence and make it harder for them to form healthy relationships. Therefore, the effects of cyber bullying may continue even after the bullying stops.
```

## Useful Conclusion Expressions

- Overall, cyber bullying can produce emotional, social, academic, and physical consequences for teenagers.
- Therefore, the effects of cyber bullying extend beyond online spaces and may influence teenagers' everyday lives.
- In conclusion, cyber bullying may harm teenagers' mental health, weaken their relationships, and reduce their academic engagement.
- These consequences show why cyber bullying is a serious issue for adolescent development and well-being.
